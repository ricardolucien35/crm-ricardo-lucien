Cold Lead Daily Follow-Up Mode of Operation

Day 0 — Initial Outreach

Send initial DM.

"Hey, I know we don't know each other, but I came across your profile and wanted to reach out. Not sure if you'd be open to it, but we're expanding and connecting with people interested in creating an additional income stream online."

No Response Sequence

Day 1 — Follow-Up #1

Follow up 24 hours later.

"Hey, just wanted to circle back and see if you were open to learning more."

Day 3 — Follow-Up #2

2 days later.

"Hey, haven't heard back from you yet. Totally okay either way — just wanted to follow up before I closed the loop on my end."

Day 6 — Follow-Up #3

3 days later.

"No worries at all. I've got some people moving forward so I need to prioritize my time, but if timing changes later feel free to reach back out."

Move to Nurture Folder

After Day 6 follow-up, move them to nurture. Tag: Nurture, Long-Term Follow-Up, Cold No Response

Nurture Follow-Up Schedule

Every 30 Days: Simple reconnect examples:

  • "Hey, hope life's been good lately. Random question — do you happen to know anyone currently looking to create an additional income stream from home?"
  • "Crazy how fast things have been moving lately. Hope everything's going well on your end."
  • "Hey, you crossed my mind today so I figured I'd say hello. Hope everything's been good."

Optional Long-Term Reactivation (Every 90–120 Days)

"I want to apologize — I've been so busy I haven't had a chance to follow up with you. How have you been?"

Presentation Follow-Up DMO

Step 1 — They Say Yes

Send the presentation. Immediately set expectation:

"Awesome. Here's the video. Take a look and let me know what stood out to you most after you watch it. Also, how soon do you think you can watch it?"

Follow-Up Window

If they give a time ("tonight", "after work", "in an hour"): Follow up 1–2 hours after expected watch time or later that evening.

First Presentation Follow-Up

"Hey, just checking in — Did you get a chance to watch it yet?"

If They Watched It — Ask This Question First

"Awesome. What did you like best?"

After they respond: Expand what THEY liked (money, freedom, flexibility, community, timing, growth). Then ask: "Why is that important to you?" or "How would that help you right now?"

Close

After conversation: "Sounds to me like you're ready to get started." Then stop talking.

If They Did NOT Watch the Video

Day 1 After No Watch

"Hey, no worries. How soon do you think you'll be able to watch it?"

Day 3 After No Watch

"Totally okay if timing's bad right now. I've just got some people that already watched it so I need to prioritize my time a bit."

Day 5–7 After No Watch

"No problem at all. I'll go ahead and close the loop for now, but if timing changes later feel free to reach back out."

Move to nurture folder after Day 5–7 no-watch follow-up. Follow up every 30–45 days.

Long-Term Nurture (Every 30–45 Days)

  • "Hey, hope things have been good lately. Random question — are you still open to creating an additional income stream at some point?"
  • "Crazy how much momentum we've had lately. Hope life's been treating you well."

Opening Message Angles

Location Angle

"Hey, I know we don't know each other, but I saw you're in Dallas. We're expanding there right now and helping people create an extra income stream from home. If you're open to taking a quick look, cool. If not, no worries."

Occupation Angle

"Hey, I know we don't know each other, but I noticed you're a realtor. We work with a lot of people in sales who are looking for an additional stream of income without interfering with what they already do. Open to taking a look?"

Intelligent Comment Angle

"Hey, I know we don't know each other, but I saw your comment on that leadership post and honestly agreed with what you said. I work with people focused on growth and entrepreneurship, so I figured I'd reach out. Would you be open to hearing more?"

Key: Not sounding overly polished or salesy. Short, calm, relevant, low-pressure messages usually outperform "perfect" scripts because they feel human.
